Common roof emergencies

  • Active roof leaks during or after a storm
  • Missing shingles after high winds
  • Storm damage around flashing, vents, or skylights
  • Water coming in around ceilings, walls, or attic areas
  • Damage from fallen branches or debris
  • Sudden gutter failures that affect water flow at the roof edge

What to do if you discover roof damage

  • Protect the inside of the home if water is actively coming in.
  • Stay clear of unsafe areas and avoid climbing onto the roof yourself.
  • Take photos if you can do so safely.
  • Call right away so the damage can be inspected and the next steps can be explained clearly.

Fast action helps limit interior damage and keeps small problems from becoming bigger, more expensive repairs.

Why fast action matters

Roof damage often gets worse when it is left alone. Water intrusion can spread into insulation, drywall, framing, and ceilings. Even when the damage looks small from the ground, leaks and flashing failures can keep causing problems until the source is addressed.

Emergency roof repair is about getting ahead of the damage, reducing stress, and figuring out whether you need a targeted repair, temporary protection, or a larger replacement plan.
